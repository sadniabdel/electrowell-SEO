Products & Compatibility

How do I know if a PLC or drive will work with my system?

Check the product specifications for:

  • Voltage requirements (120V, 240V, 480V, etc.)
  • Communication protocols (Ethernet/IP, Modbus, Profibus, etc.)
  • I/O count and types
  • Programming software compatibility

Need help? Our technical team can assist with compatibility questions.

Are all products brand new?

Yes, all products are brand new unless specifically marked as refurbished or used. We only sell genuine products from authorized distributors.

Do products come with manufacturer warranties?

Yes, all products include full manufacturer warranties. Warranty periods vary by product (typically 1-2 years). See individual product pages for details.

Can you help me select the right VFD for my motor?

Yes! We need to know:

  • Motor horsepower (HP) or kilowatts (kW)
  • Voltage (single or three-phase)
  • Full load amps (FLA)
  • Application type (pump, fan, conveyor, etc.)

Contact our technical team for VFD selection assistance.

What's the difference between Allen-Bradley and Siemens PLCs?

Both are industry leaders with different strengths:

  • Allen-Bradley (Rockwell): Popular in North America, RSLogix/Studio 5000 programming, strong in discrete manufacturing
  • Siemens: Popular in Europe, TIA Portal programming, strong in process automation

Choice often depends on existing systems, programmer familiarity, and regional support.

Do you sell programming cables and software?

We sell programming cables. Software licenses must typically be purchased directly from manufacturers (Rockwell, Siemens, etc.).

What programming software do I need?

Depends on the PLC brand:

  • Allen-Bradley: RSLogix 500/5000 or Studio 5000
  • Siemens: TIA Portal or Step 7
  • Omron: CX-Programmer or Sysmac Studio
  • Schneider: Unity Pro or EcoStruxure

How do I wire a VFD?

Safety Warning: VFD installation should only be performed by qualified electricians. Basic steps:

  1. Disconnect all power sources
  2. Connect input power (L1, L2, L3)
  3. Connect motor output (U, V, W)
  4. Wire control terminals as needed
  5. Connect ground properly
  6. Follow manufacturer's manual

Always follow local electrical codes and manufacturer guidelines.

What's the difference between a PLC and a PAC?

  • PLC (Programmable Logic Controller): Designed for discrete control, ladder logic programming
  • PAC (Programmable Automation Controller): More powerful, handles discrete + analog + motion, multiple programming languages

PACs are essentially advanced PLCs with more capabilities.
